PC Cases

Full Tower Cases

Size:
Largest of the standard form factors, typically measuring around 22 x 9 x 22 inches (559 x 229 x 559 mm).
Compatible Motherboards:
Accommodates standard ATX, microATX (mATX), and extended ATX (E-ATX) motherboards.
Design:
Offers expansive space for high-end components and elaborate cooling setups.
May include multiple drive bays, fan mounts, and liquid cooling radiator support.
Building Considerations:
Spacious interior allows for easy component installation and cable management.
Supports multiple GPUs, large CPU coolers, and extensive storage configurations.
Ideal for extreme gaming rigs, high-performance workstations, and servers requiring maximum expandability.
Perfect for enthusiasts and content creators who need ample space for multiple GPUs, storage drives, and custom cooling solutions.
Design Complexity:
Provides a canvas for advanced and intricate builds, ideal for enthusiasts and power users.
May feature modular designs for customizable layouts and advanced cable routing options.
Availability and Pricing:
Varies in availability depending on brand and model, with a range of features and price points.
Often considered a premium option, offering top-tier build quality and advanced features.

Mid Tower Cases

Size:
Most popular choice for gaming and enthusiast builds, typically measuring around 18 x 8 x 20 inches (457 x 203 x 508 mm).
Compatible Motherboards:
Accommodates standard ATX motherboards.
Supports microATX (mATX) motherboards for a slightly smaller build.
Design:
Provides a good balance between size and functionality.
Offers sleek and modern aesthetics with various styles and finishes.
Building Considerations:
Easy to work with for builders, allowing for organized and efficient component installation.
Spacious interior with cable management options for clean and tidy builds.
Ideal for gaming setups and enthusiast builds requiring a balance of size and functionality.
Perfect for users who want ample space for components like GPUs, cooling systems, and storage drives without the bulk of a full tower case.
Design Complexity:
Offers a straightforward build process for beginners and experienced builders alike.
May feature tool-less drive bays and removable panels for easy access.
Availability and Pricing:
One of the most widely available case sizes, offering a range of brands and models.
Comes in various price points to suit different budgets, from budget-friendly options to premium models with advanced features.

Small Form Factor Cases

Size Range:
Vary from mini tower size to Mini-ITX (m-ITX) sizes.
Compatible Motherboards:
Mostly fit m-ITX motherboards, with some exceptions extending to microATX (mATX).
Design:
Designed to fit very little, emphasizing compactness.
Building Considerations:
Requires extensive planning, organization, component knowledge, and cable management.
Ideal for ultra-compact desktop systems and HTPCs (Home Theater PCs) with extremely limited space.
Perfect for users who prioritize portability, space-saving, and building unique, custom systems.
Design Complexity:
Due to smaller size, SFF cases require more in-depth design and compatibility consideration.
Availability and Pricing:
May be more expensive due to specialized design and limited production.
Components like the motherboard, cooler, power supply, and compatible graphics card may be challenging to find at lower prices.
